Karachi driving test: what to expect & how to pass first time.

Quick answer: The Karachi driving test (Test of Competence) has two parts — a 25-question theory exam and a practical driving test covering parallel parking, 3-point turn, hill start (manual), and a short city drive. You need 17 of 25 correct to clear the theory, and a clean run on the practical to pass. Most first-time failures happen on parallel parking. Total time on-site: 2–3 hours. Test fee is included in the Rs 1,410 (5-year) or Rs 1,860 (10-year) permanent licence fee.

The two-part structure

The DLS Sindh practical — officially the Test of Competence — is run in two back-to-back parts, usually on the same morning. You take theory first at a computer terminal; clear it, and the examiner calls you for the practical.

Fail the theory and you are sent home the same day — the practical does not start. Fail the practical and the theory result does carry over: on your retake you only redo the practical.

Part 1: The 25-question theory test

The theory is a computer-based multiple-choice exam conducted at the DLS centre. Each question has 3 or 4 options. You select one and click Next. There is usually a 15–20 minute soft time limit, though most candidates finish inside 10 minutes.

Topic distribution (approximate)

  • Road signs — 10 of 25. Mandatory (blue circles), prohibitory (red circles), warning (red triangles), informative (rectangular blue). See our Pakistan Road Signs Guide.
  • Right-of-way & intersections — 5 of 25. Who goes first at 4-way stops, T-junctions, uncontrolled roundabouts.
  • Speed limits & lane rules — 4 of 25. Urban 50 km/h, highway 80–120 km/h, motorway 120 km/h.
  • Signals, indicators, horn use — 3 of 25. When, how far in advance, urban vs highway rules.
  • NHSO-2000 basics & documentation — 3 of 25. Seatbelt law, helmet rule, licence types, minimum age.

Language: you can select English or Urdu at the start. The Urdu translation is straightforward and often clearer for local candidates — use whichever you are most comfortable with.

Part 2: The practical driving test

Most Karachi DLS centres run the practical inside a controlled testing area (marked bays, cones, ramps), followed by a brief on-road drive in the vicinity. The five standard tasks:

2.1 Controls demonstration

Before you start the engine, the examiner will ask you to operate indicators (left & right), headlights (low and high beam), horn, hazard lights, and wipers. Miss any and you may be asked to restart. Take 20 seconds — don't rush.

2.2 Hill start or ramp (manual only)

If you're tested on a manual car (Suzuki Swift, Mehran, older Corolla), you'll be asked to stop on a slight incline and restart without rolling back. Coordination of clutch, handbrake, and accelerator is what's being scored. If you're in an automatic (Alto 2021, newer Cultus), this step is skipped.

2.3 Parallel parking

Two cones or a simulated car gap, usually about 1.5 times the length of your car. You have two attempts with one correction before it's marked as failed. The Karachi practical bay is standardised at 5.5 to 6 metres — the exact reference-point method is in our Parallel Parking Guide.

2.4 Three-point turn

On a strip about the width of a DHA internal road. Turn the car 180 degrees using exactly three moves: forward, reverse, forward. Examiners watch for shoulder checks and smooth steering. Don't stall — that's an instant mark.

2.5 Short on-road drive

The examiner directs you on a 500m to 1km loop on the public street outside the DLS centre. You're assessed on lane discipline, signal use before turns, obedience of stop signs and signals, and safe speed. Roll through a stop, cut a lane, or miss an indicator and you'll be marked down.

What examiners are actually looking for

Examiners are not looking for perfection — they are looking for a safe, aware, unhurried driver. Our instructors, who have observed hundreds of students go through the test, consistently report the same five "watch-fors":

  1. Mirror checks. Before every lane change, every turn, every reverse. Exaggerate them — examiners need to see you checking.
  2. Signal timing. At least 3 seconds before the manoeuvre. Not during, not after.
  3. Smooth clutch/accelerator. No kangaroo hops, no stalling. Especially on the hill start.
  4. Steering control. Hand-over-hand on tight turns, returned to 10-and-2 (or 9-and-3) on straights.
  5. Calm under instruction. If the examiner asks "take the next left," acknowledge with "okay" or a nod. Don't panic-reply.

Instructor line: "Slow is smooth. Smooth is safe. Safe is pass."

Roman Urdu: "Aaram se chalao, smooth rahay. Smooth raho, safe rahogi. Safe ho gayi to pass ho gayi."

7 most common failure reasons

  1. Parallel parking — too many corrections. You get 2 attempts with 1 correction. Third correction = fail.
  2. Stalling during hill start. Rolling back more than half a metre without handbrake engagement is a fail.
  3. Forgetting to signal before the 3-point turn. It counts as a lane manoeuvre.
  4. Rolling stops. A pause of less than 2 seconds at a stop sign is a rolling stop — mark against you.
  5. Mirror blindness. Lane changes without a visible mirror check. Fail.
  6. Over-reliance on the instructor's voice during practice. Students who only drive when told where to turn freeze on the test's independent segment.
  7. Panic on the theory — not reading all options. Two options often look similar; read all four before answering.

Don't rely on luck on test day.

Test day: hour-by-hour plan

  • T-24 hours: Drive 30–45 minutes the day before. Light practice — parallel parking, hill start, 3-point turn. Don't cram new skills.
  • T-12 hours: Lay out your documents — CNIC, learner's permit, appointment slip, photos, fee. Charge your phone.
  • T-2 hours: Eat a proper breakfast. Bananas, eggs, paratha. Hydrate but don't over-caffeinate.
  • T-45 min: Arrive at the DLS centre. Locate the fee counter first.
  • T-0: Report to the verification window. Submit documents. Get seated for theory.
  • T+30 min: Theory results announced. Move to practical queue.
  • T+60–120 min: Practical. Breathe. Follow instructions. Ask for clarification in Urdu if you need to.
  • T+3 hours: Results. If you passed — congratulations. Collect your licence or wait 7–14 days for postal delivery.

Seekho instructor tips for passing first time

  1. Practise at the DLS bay the week before. The testing lot is often quiet on off-days — drive past, look at the exact cone spacing.
  2. Book a morning slot. 8:30–10:30am is before the branch backs up.
  3. Wear something you can comfortably drive in. Loose kameez, closed shoes, no heels.
  4. Keep both hands on the wheel unless shifting. Examiners notice hand position.
  5. Say "check" out loud during mirror checks. It signals your awareness audibly.
  6. After you finish each task, wait for the examiner's next instruction. Don't anticipate.
  7. If you make a small mistake, recover calmly. One slip does not fail you — panicking does.
