How to get a driving licence in Karachi (2026 DLS Sindh guide).

Quick answer: To get a driving licence in Karachi, apply for a learner's permit at your nearest DLS Sindh centre for Rs 100, wait the mandatory 42 days, then book a test at dlsonline.sindhpolice.gov.pk. The permanent licence costs Rs 1,410 for 5 years or Rs 1,860 for 10 years and requires passing a 25-question theory test plus a practical driving test. Bring your CNIC, two photos, a medical fitness certificate, and your learner's permit.

Who needs a driving licence in Karachi

If you drive a car, motorcycle, or light transport vehicle on any public road in Karachi — including DHA, Clifton, PECHS, Gulshan, or the Shahrah-e-Faisal corridor — you legally need a valid driving licence issued by the Directorate of Licensing (DLS) Sindh, a unit of Sindh Police.

Driving without a licence can mean a fine under the National Highway Safety Ordinance 2000, impoundment of the vehicle, or an FIR if you are involved in an accident. Even learners practising with a relative in an empty Korangi plot are technically required to hold a learner's permit.

English: You must be at least 18 to apply for a car licence. 16 for a motorcycle.

Roman Urdu: Car ki licence ke liye kam az kam 18 saal honay chahiye. Motorcycle ke liye 16.

The two-stage DLS Sindh process

Pakistan uses a two-stage licensing system. You cannot walk into a DLS office and leave with a full licence the same day — the 42-day waiting period is deliberately built in so new drivers accumulate supervised road time before being tested.

  1. Learner's licence (permit): Issued same-day. Valid 6 months. Lets you practise on public roads while supervised by a licensed driver.
  2. Permanent licence: Issued after passing theory + practical tests. Valid 5 or 10 years depending on the fee tier you choose.

Step 1: Get your learner's permit (Rs 100)

The learner's permit is the cheapest and fastest part of the whole process. You can walk into any DLS Sindh centre in Karachi and, with the right documents, walk out with a permit the same afternoon.

What you do on the day

  1. Arrive at the DLS centre with your documents (see §Documents).
  2. Collect an application form (or fill it in at the online portal and print it).
  3. Deposit the Rs 100 fee at the bank counter inside the DLS complex.
  4. Submit your form, photos, CNIC copies, and fee receipt at the verification window.
  5. Provide a live biometric (thumbprint + photo) — usually takes under 5 minutes.
  6. Collect your learner's permit card (some branches print same-day; others mail within 7–14 days).

Total time on-site: 1.5 to 3 hours, mostly queuing. Go early — before 10am — for the shortest wait.

Pro tip from our instructors

The Clifton DLS centre is usually faster than the Garden Traffic Headquarters. If you live in DHA or Clifton, it's worth the 10-minute drive.

Step 2: The 42-day waiting period

Once your learner's permit is issued, DLS Sindh will not let you book a permanent-licence test for 42 calendar days. The rule cannot legally be skipped. There is no "quick process" agent who can bypass it — if anyone offers to, they are running a scam.

The 42 days are your window to build real driving competence. Most Seekho students use this time to finish behind-the-wheel lessons, sit the Saturday theory workshop, and put in 15–20 hours of supervised practice. By day 42, you are test-ready, not test-anxious.

Don't waste the 42 days

We see students book the permanent-licence test on day 43 without having driven since day 1. They fail the practical and have to rebook — wasted fee, wasted time, and a confidence dent. Use the 42 days.

Step 3: The 25-question theory test

The theory exam is a 25-question multiple-choice paper covering road signs, right-of-way rules, speed limits, signals, and basic traffic law. You need at least 17 correct answers (~70 percent) to pass.

Topics you'll be tested on:

  • Road signs — mandatory (blue circle), prohibitory (red circle with slash), warning (red triangle), informative (rectangular blue).
  • Right-of-way — intersections, roundabouts, uncontrolled junctions.
  • Speed limits — urban 50 km/h, highway 80–120 km/h, motorway 120 km/h.
  • Signals and indicators — when to use them, how far in advance.
  • NHSO-2000 basics — seatbelt law, helmet law, blood alcohol rule.

We cover every likely question in our Saturday theory workshop, and Seekho's downloadable Practice Test — 100 Q True/False matches the exam format closely. Students who drill both usually walk in and finish in under 10 minutes.

Step 4: The practical driving test

After you clear theory, you drive for the examiner. At most Karachi DLS centres the practical is conducted in a dedicated testing area and covers:

  1. Vehicle controls check — examiner asks you to demonstrate indicators, headlights, horn, and wipers.
  2. Hill start or ramp — clutch-and-handbrake coordination (manual only).
  3. Reverse parking / parallel parking — the single most-failed section; see our Parallel Parking Guide.
  4. 3-point turn — on a narrow strip.
  5. Short on-road drive — follow examiner's directions, obey signs and signals, demonstrate lane discipline.

Full details including examiner criteria are in our Karachi Driving Test guide.

Documents you need to bring

The paperwork is the silent killer of first attempts. Miss one document and you get turned away — bring everything on this list, in physical form, on the day.

For the learner's permit

  • Original CNIC (plus two photocopies).
  • Two passport-size photographs (blue or white background).
  • Medical fitness certificate from a registered medical practitioner — must include eyesight test.
  • Proof of residence — utility bill in your name or a parent/guardian's name.
  • Fee of Rs 100 in cash.

For the permanent licence

  • Everything above, plus —
  • Original learner's permit (must be more than 42 days old).
  • Appointment slip printed from dlsonline.sindhpolice.gov.pk.
  • Fee of Rs 1,410 (5-year) or Rs 1,860 (10-year) in cash or challan.

Karachi DLS office locations

Karachi has multiple DLS Sindh offices so you don't have to travel across the city. Exact addresses and appointment availability vary — confirm on the DLS online portal. TBD-faryal-verify full branch list.

  • Traffic Headquarters (Garden): Main DLS centre, near Karachi Zoo. Busiest; longest queues.
  • Clifton DLS centre: Near Clifton Block 5. Popular with DHA and Clifton residents.
  • Gulshan-e-Iqbal DLS: Near UP Morr. Convenient for PECHS, Nazimabad, Gulshan.
  • Malir DLS: Serves Malir, Korangi, Landhi, Shah Faisal Colony.
  • North Nazimabad DLS: For North Nazimabad, Nazimabad, New Karachi residents.

When you book your appointment online, you select the branch. Always double-check the location printed on your slip before travelling on test day.

Using the DLS online portal

The Sindh Police licensing portal (dlsonline.sindhpolice.gov.pk) lets you book appointments, download forms, pay challans, and check licence status without visiting the centre twice.

Create an account with your CNIC and mobile number. Verify via OTP. From the dashboard you can:

  • Apply for a learner's licence and choose your branch + time slot.
  • Apply for the permanent licence after your 42-day wait.
  • Apply for a renewal, duplicate, or category upgrade.
  • Download the fee challan and pay at any partner bank (usually Sindh Bank or NBP).

The portal is functional but not fast. Use a desktop if you can — mobile drops fields during long sessions.

Common mistakes to avoid

  1. Showing up without a medical certificate. The DLS will not begin your file without one. Visit a clinic the same morning if you need to.
  2. Booking your permanent-licence test on day 43 with no practice. Failure rate on first attempt is high — rebooking costs time and mental energy.
  3. Using an expired CNIC. Renew NADRA first. Even a CNIC expired by one day gets rejected.
  4. Trusting an agent to "fix" your licence. If anyone asks for a large cash payment beyond the official fee, walk away. It's illegal and the licence may be void.
  5. Forgetting to bring fresh photos. Old photocopied ones are rejected. Bring a fresh strip from any Saddar or Clifton photo studio.
  6. Driving without the physical card. Your permit must be on you when you drive. A photo on your phone is not sufficient under Sindh Police interpretation.
