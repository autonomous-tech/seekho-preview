Pakistan driving fines: NHSO-2000 violations, amounts & the challan process.

Quick answer: Pakistani driving fines are set by the National Highway Safety Ordinance 2000 (NHSO-2000) and harmonised provincial rules. Fines range from Rs 200 for minor violations (no indicator, broken tail light) to Rs 5,000+ for severe violations (drunk driving, dangerous driving, repeat offences). You can pay online via ePay, at a partner bank, or at the Traffic Police office. Disputed challans are contested within 15 days at the Traffic Magistrate. Below is the full fine schedule, categorised by severity, plus step-by-step instructions for paying and contesting a Karachi traffic challan. What is NHSO-2000?

The National Highway Safety Ordinance 2000 is Pakistan's foundational traffic-law framework. It defines offences, assigns penalties, sets the licensing authority, and empowers the National Highways & Motorway Police (NH&MP) to enforce on federal roads.

Provincial traffic forces — Sindh Traffic Police in Karachi, Punjab Traffic Police in Lahore, Islamabad Traffic Police in the capital — use a harmonised fine schedule derived from NHSO-2000 for provincial and urban roads. Amounts can vary slightly by jurisdiction and change by notification. The figures below are current guidance as of 2026 and should be double-checked against the latest Sindh Police notification before any dispute.

The 3 severity tiers

Minor

Paperwork / courtesy violations

Rs 200 – 500

Indicator off, broken tail light, missing fitness certificate, tinted windows. Usually a same-day fix + challan.

Major

Moving violations with risk

Rs 500 – 2,000

Red-light jumping, overspeeding, wrong-side driving, mobile phone use. Routine enforcement target.

Severe

Reckless / criminal-adjacent

Rs 2,000+

Drunk driving, dangerous driving, driving without licence, racing. Often plus licence suspension or FIR.

Repeat-offender multiplier

NHSO-2000 allows fines to be doubled for a second offence within 12 months. A third offence can mean licence suspension for a period determined by the Traffic Magistrate. Track your challan history at the ePay portal so you don't cross the threshold unknowingly.

How challans are issued

In Karachi you can receive a challan three ways:

  1. On-the-spot stop. A traffic officer pulls you over, writes a physical challan slip, and hands it to you. You keep one carbon copy; the officer keeps another; a third goes to the station.
  2. E-challan (camera / ANPR). Auto-enforcement cameras on Shahrah-e-Faisal, Korangi Road, and major Clifton/DHA junctions photograph your plate and match it to your CNIC via the vehicle registration. No physical stop. You are notified by SMS and/or by mail.
  3. Motorway e-challan. NH&MP operates speed-monitoring cameras on the M-9, Indus Highway, and other federal roads. Same auto-process as urban cameras but routed through the federal system.

How to pay a traffic challan in Karachi

1. Online via ePay (fastest)

  1. Visit the ePay Sindh portal or the 1Link ePay app.
  2. Select Traffic Challan as the service.
  3. Enter your CNIC or challan number.
  4. Confirm the violation and amount displayed.
  5. Pay via bank card, mobile wallet (JazzCash, Easypaisa, NayaPay), or internet banking.
  6. Save the receipt. Payment should clear the challan from the system within 24–48 hours.

2. At a partner bank counter

Walk into any branch of National Bank of Pakistan (NBP), Sindh Bank, or HBL. Give the teller your challan number or CNIC. Pay cash. Keep the stamped deposit slip.

3. Cash at the Traffic Police challan office

The challan office is listed on your ticket. Bring your CNIC, original challan slip, and cash. Receive a stamped receipt — keep it for at least 6 months.

How to contest a challan

If you believe a challan was wrongly issued — mistaken identity, faulty camera reading, or a technical defence — you have 15 days from the issue date to contest.

  1. Visit the Traffic Police challan office listed on the ticket.
  2. File a written objection. State the violation cited, why you contest it, and attach evidence (dashcam clip, photos, witness statement, GPS data from a mapping app).
  3. Request a hearing before the Traffic Magistrate.
  4. Attend the hearing on the scheduled date. Present your evidence calmly. The magistrate decides on the spot.
  5. If the magistrate rules in your favour, the challan is cancelled. Keep the cancellation order until the system reflects it.

Lawyers: not required

You do not need a lawyer for a traffic contest. Self-representation is standard. Bring evidence, stay respectful, present in 2 minutes or less.

Check outstanding challans against your CNIC

Before you buy a used car, renew your licence, or pay vehicle token tax, check for outstanding challans on the CNIC of the registered owner:

  • ePay Sindh portal — enter CNIC → view all pending challans.
  • Motor Vehicle Department Sindh (mvd.sindh.gov.pk) — vehicle-tax checker shows outstanding fines against a plate number.
  • SMS: send your CNIC to the official Sindh Traffic Police number printed on their vehicles.

Unpaid challans block token-tax renewal and some vehicle transfers. Clear them before attempting the transaction.
