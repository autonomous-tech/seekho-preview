Pakistan road signs: complete guide with Urdu translations.

Quick answer: Pakistani road signs follow four categories from the Vienna Convention — mandatory (blue circles) tell you what you must do, prohibitory (red circles) tell you what you cannot do, warning (red triangles) flag hazards ahead, and informative (blue rectangles) give guidance like hospitals, petrol pumps, and exits. This guide covers 40+ commonly encountered signs on Karachi, Sindh, and motorway roads — with their meanings, Urdu names, and what they cost you in fines if ignored.

The 4 categories of Pakistani road signs

Pakistan is a signatory to the 1968 Vienna Convention on Road Signs and Signals, which standardises shapes and colours across countries. If you've driven in the UAE, Turkey, or Malaysia, the signs in Karachi will feel familiar. The categories:

Shape tells you the category:

Circle (blue) = MUST · Circle (red) = DO NOT · Triangle (red) = CAREFUL · Rectangle (blue/green) = INFO

Mandatory signs (blue circles)

↑

Blue circle — "you must"

Instructs you to perform the indicated action (direction, minimum speed, required lane).

↑

Go Straight Ahead

Proceed only straight at this point — no turning.

→

Turn Right

Must turn right — no straight through.

←

Turn Left

Left mor lein

Must turn left — no straight or right.

↱

Turn Right Ahead

Next available turn must be right.

↶

Roundabout / Keep Left

Follow roundabout direction counter-clockwise.

Kam az kam 50 km/h

Motorway and highway — must maintain at least this speed.

🚲

Cyclists Only Route

Sirf cycle ke liye

Rare in Karachi. Common on university campuses.

🚶

Pedestrian Crossing Required

Pedal ghaadi aur paidal ke liye

Shared footway — drive slowly and give way.

Warning signs (red triangles)

Red triangle — "hazard ahead"

Alerts you to something on the road you need to prepare for — rarely a ban, always a reduction in confidence.

Sharp Left Bend

Aagay tez left mor

Slow down — a sharp left turn approaches.

Sharp Right Bend

Aagay tez right mor

Slow down — a sharp right turn approaches.

Double Bend

Do mor aagay

Common on Clifton Drive and Mai Kolachi curves.

School Ahead

School ke paas

Children likely. Reduce speed to 25 km/h.

Speed Bump / Hump

Speed breaker aa raha

Frequent on Karachi residential streets.

Steep Descent

Utarai aa rahi

Downhill gradient — check brakes, use lower gear.

Steep Climb

Charhai aa rahi

Uphill — rare in flat Karachi; seen near Northern Bypass.

Crossroads Ahead

Chauraha aa raha

Uncontrolled intersection — yield to right.

T-Junction Ahead

T-junction aa raha

Road ends in a T-shape — prepare to turn left or right.

Roundabout Ahead

Circle aa raha

Approach slowly, yield to traffic in the circle.

Other Danger

Khatra — aram se chalaiye

Generic hazard — examine what's ahead.

Level Crossing

Train ki line

Railway crossing. Stop if barrier is down.

Road Narrows

Road tang ho rahi

Common on DHA residential streets.

Slippery Road

Phisalti hui road

Wet road, oil spill, or loose surface ahead.

Informative signs (blue rectangles)

INFO

Blue/green rectangle — "this way to…"

Guidance. Motorway exits, city names, hospitals, petrol pumps, parking, telephone.

H

Hospital

Hospital ka rasta

Sometimes with red cross. Common near Liaquat National, AKUH.

⛽

Petrol Station

Petrol pump

Distance often on supplementary plate.

P

Parking

Parking yahan

Authorised parking zone.

📞

Emergency Phone

Emergency phone

Motorway helpbox.

EXIT

Motorway Exit

Motorway ka exit

Typically lists city name (Hyderabad, Nooriabad).

✈

Airport

Airport ka rasta

Jinnah International near Star Gate.

🚶

Pedestrian Crossing

Paidal crossing

Zebra crossing — drivers must yield.

🏪

Services / Rest Area

Rest area

Food, fuel, rest. Common on M-9.

Special regulatory signs (Stop, Yield)

STOP

Stop Sign (Octagon)

Rukiye — full stop

Red octagon. Full 2-second stop required. Rolling stop = DLS test fail. Usually written in English + Urdu.

Yield / Give Way

Rasta do

Inverted red triangle. Slow down and give way to cross traffic. You don't have to stop if the road is clear.

GREEN

Traffic Signal

Green light = jao

Standard red/amber/green. Running a red is a major fine under NHSO-2000.

!

Flashing Amber

Amber light = dekhke jao

Proceed with caution. Signal not in operation.

Lane markings and painted signs

Not every instruction is on a post. Much of Karachi driving comes from what's painted on the road itself:

  • Solid white line: Do not cross. Usually on motorways or near bends.
  • Broken white line: Safe to change lanes. Karachi's default.
  • Double yellow lines: No overtaking. Rare but present on winding stretches of the M-9.
  • Yellow kerb paint: No parking. DHA commercial streets.
  • Zebra crossing (thick white stripes): Pedestrians have priority.
  • Painted arrows in lanes: That lane must go in that direction at the next intersection.
  • Red-painted kerb: Bus/emergency-service zone — no parking, no stopping.

Karachi-specific signs you'll see

Outside the national standard set, there are a few Karachi-specific signs and markings worth knowing:

  • DHA entry gates: Speed-humped approaches with "Welcome to Phase X — Speed limit 40 km/h" boards. Strictly enforced.
  • Sindh Police check-posts: Flashing amber beacons and "SLOW" painted on road. Reduce to 20 km/h.
  • "Speed Breaker Ahead" yellow boards on residential streets — often without a warning triangle.
  • "One Way" painted arrows on Clifton and PECHS narrow streets — check direction before turning.
  • Hospital silent-zone boards near AKUH, Liaquat National, Indus Hospital. No horn.
  • "School Ahead" yellow boards near St Patrick's, Habib Public, BVS, Foundation schools.

DLS Sindh theory test preparation

The 25-question DLS Sindh theory test dedicates roughly 10 of 25 questions to road signs. Focus your drill on the prohibitory and warning categories — they carry the most test weight because a wrong interpretation on a real road causes most accidents.

  1. Learn the shape → category rule first. Everything else follows.
  2. For prohibitory signs, memorise: No Entry, No Parking, No U-Turn, No Horn, No Overtaking, Speed Limit, End of Restriction, No Stopping.
  3. For warning signs: Sharp Bend, School, Speed Bump, Roundabout Ahead, Level Crossing, Road Narrows.
