Car dashboard warning lights: meaning, severity, action.

Quick answer: Car dashboard warning lights are colour-coded — red means stop as soon as safe (engine, oil, brake, coolant faults), amber or yellow means caution (check engine, ABS, tyre pressure, airbag — drive to a mechanic within 24–48 hours), and green or blue means information (headlights, indicators, cruise control — no fault). Ignoring a red light can seize your engine or disable your brakes. This guide covers every common icon you'll see in a Suzuki Swift, Alto, Cultus, Toyota Corolla, or Honda City — with Roman Urdu names and the right action for each.

The colour code: red, amber, green

Every modern car — whether a 2016 Suzuki Swift, a 2021 Alto, or a new Toyota Corolla — uses the same international colour code for dashboard warning lights. It's worth memorising, because in the 0.8 seconds between noticing a light and deciding what to do, the colour tells you almost everything you need.

Red = stop

Critical fault. Pull over safely, turn off engine, call a mechanic.

Amber = caution

Fault exists. Drive to a mechanic today or tomorrow.

Green / blue = info

System active. No fault. Just telling you something is on.

The one rule

If a light changes colour while driving — amber to red, or a new red light appears — pull over within 500 metres. Most Karachi mechanics charge less for a diagnostic than a single engine repair caused by ignoring a red light for "just five more minutes".

Red warning lights — stop immediately

If you see any of these lights, find the nearest safe spot, turn on hazards, and pull over. In Karachi that means the curb of a quiet service road, not the middle lane of Shahrah-e-Faisal.

⛽

Engine Oil Pressure

Oil pressure ki warning

An oil-can icon with a drip. Oil pressure has dropped. The engine can seize within 2–5 minutes of continued driving.

ActionPull over. Turn off engine. Check oil with dipstick. If low, top up and drive to mechanic. If not, call a tow.
🌡

Engine Temperature / Coolant

Engine garam ho rahi hai

A thermometer over waves. Coolant is too hot or too low. Ignoring causes head-gasket failure — the most expensive single engine repair.

ActionPull over. Let engine cool 20 minutes. Check coolant reservoir. Top up with water in emergency, proper coolant at a workshop.
🔋

Battery / Charging

Battery ki warning

A battery icon with + and −. The alternator is no longer charging the battery. You have 15–30 minutes of driving on stored charge before electricals die.

ActionDrive straight home or to a mechanic. Turn off A/C, stereo, and headlights if safe to preserve charge.
🅟

Brake System

Brake ki warning

A circle with ! inside, or the word "BRAKE". Often the handbrake is still engaged — check first. If handbrake is off, brake fluid is low or a fault exists.

ActionConfirm handbrake is released. If light stays on, check brake-fluid reservoir. If you feel soft pedal, do not drive.
🔴

Seat Belt Reminder

Seat belt pehno

A person with a belt across the chest. You or a passenger hasn't fastened a seatbelt. Under NHSO-2000 not wearing one is also a traffic fine.

ActionFasten the belt. Light turns off.
🚪

Door Ajar

Darwaza khula hai

Car outline with open doors. A door or the bonnet isn't fully closed.

ActionPull over. Close all doors firmly. Check bonnet latch.
🅿

Parking Brake

Handbrake lagi hai

A circle with P or !. Your handbrake is still engaged. Driving with it up burns rear brakes in under 10 minutes.

ActionRelease handbrake fully. If light persists, brake fluid may be low.
🛢

Low Fuel (sometimes red, usually amber)

Petrol khatam ho raha hai

A pump icon. You have typically 40–60 km of range left in a Swift or Corolla; about 30 km in an Alto.

ActionFill up at the next petrol pump. Don't let the tank run dry — the fuel pump is cooled by petrol.

Amber / yellow warning lights — caution, not emergency

You can keep driving, but book a diagnostic within 24 to 48 hours. Ignoring amber lights for weeks is how a Rs 500 sensor replacement becomes a Rs 50,000 catalytic converter.

🔧

Check Engine

Engine check ki warning

A yellow engine-block outline. Your engine-management computer detected a fault — commonly a loose fuel cap, O2 sensor, or emissions issue.

ActionTighten fuel cap first (5 percent of cases fixed instantly). If it persists, get OBD-II scan. Rs 300–500 at most Karachi workshops.
🛞

TPMS — Tyre Pressure

Tyre mein hawa kam hai

A horseshoe with an ! inside. One or more tyres is at least 25% below recommended pressure.

ActionDrive slowly to a petrol pump. Fill to PSI listed on driver-door sticker (32–35 PSI typical). Light clears within 1 km.
ABS

Anti-lock Braking System

ABS ki fault

ABS in a circle. ABS is disabled — brakes still work but will lock during hard stops.

ActionDrive gently. Avoid hard braking. Book diagnostic within a week. Wheel speed sensor most common cause.
🛡

Airbag / SRS

Airbag system ki warning

A person with a circular airbag. The airbag system is disabled — airbags will not deploy in a crash.

ActionDrive carefully, avoid risky manoeuvres. Book dealership scan within a week. Rs 2,000–5,000 in Karachi.
🛑

Traction Control

Traction control off

A car with wavy lines beneath it, or "TCS" / "ESP OFF". Skid control is disabled or faulted.

ActionIf you switched it off, press button to re-enable. If it won't re-enable, book a diagnostic.
💨

Glow Plug (diesel only)

Glow plug garam ho raha hai

A coiled-filament icon. Diesel pre-heat. Wait for it to turn off before cranking.

ActionWait 3–10 seconds before turning the key to start. Normal in cold weather.
💧

Washer Fluid Low

Wiper ka paani khatam

A windscreen with drops. Washer reservoir is low.

ActionTop up with water + a drop of dish soap. Rs 50 at any petrol pump shop.
🌫

DPF / Emissions (diesel)

Exhaust filter bhara hai

Common on modern diesel. Particulate filter is partly clogged. Needs a regen drive at highway speed.

ActionDrive at 80+ km/h on Northern Bypass or Motorway for 20 minutes. If light persists, see a mechanic.

Green & blue info lights — no fault

These lights are there to tell you something is on, not that something is wrong. No action needed unless you want to switch the feature off.

💡

Headlights (low beam)

Headlights on hain

Green headlamp icon. Low-beam headlights are on.

Info onlyUse at dusk, night, and during rain or fog.
💡

High Beam

High beam / full light on hai

Blue headlamp with horizontal lines. High beam is active.

Info onlyDip to low beam when a car approaches within 150 metres — standard courtesy.
◀▶

Turn Indicator

Indicator chal raha hai

A blinking green arrow. Your turn indicator is on.

Info onlyCancel manually after lane changes if the car doesn't auto-cancel.
⬇

Cruise Control

Cruise control set hai

A speedometer arrow, sometimes with "SET" or "CRUISE". Cruise control is active or ready.

Info onlyTap brake to disengage instantly. Not recommended on urban Karachi roads.
🔄

Auto Start-Stop

Engine auto-off feature

An "A" inside a circular arrow. The eco feature is running. Engine stops at red lights and restarts when you press the accelerator.

Info onlyPress the button with a crossed-out "A" to disable for the current drive.
☔

Rear Fog Light

Back fog light on hai

Amber lamp with slashes going down. Rear fog is on.

Info onlyUse only in visibility below 100m — dazzles drivers behind otherwise.

When multiple lights come on at once

If three or more warning lights come on at the same time — especially battery + engine check + ABS — don't panic but don't ignore it. It almost always means one of two things:

  1. Low voltage. A failing alternator or dying battery isn't feeding enough power to the car's computers. Every system throws a warning.
  2. A bad ECU scan / sensor loss. The main electronic control unit has lost communication with a sensor cluster.

Pull over, turn the engine off completely, wait two minutes, restart. If the lights come back:

  • Don't drive on Karachi highways (Shahrah-e-Faisal, Super Highway, Northern Bypass) — you could lose power steering or brake boost if the alternator fails mid-drive.
  • Call a mechanic. In DHA and Clifton, home-visit mechanics via WhatsApp typically run Rs 1,500 to Rs 3,000.
  • Avoid roadside "electrician stalls" for alternator work — go to a reputable workshop.

The 30-second startup check

Every professional driver we train at Seekho does the same thing before moving the car:

  1. Key in, ignition to ACC (not start). All warning lights should illuminate briefly — this tests the bulbs.
  2. Start engine. All warning lights should turn off within 3 seconds.
  3. Anything still lit? Don't drive until you know what it is.
  4. Gear to neutral (manual) or P (auto). Handbrake off. Seatbelt on. Mirrors checked, indicator set, move.

English: "If a warning light is still on after the engine starts, assume the worst until proven wrong."

Roman Urdu: "Engine start karne ke baad jo light on reh jaye, usay serious samjho — tab tak jab tak mechanic na keh de sab theek hai."

Roman Urdu glossary

Useful for explaining problems to a mechanic over WhatsApp:

  • "Engine check ki warning aa rahi hai" — Check-engine light is on.
  • "Engine garam ho rahi hai" — Engine overheating.
  • "Oil light jal rahi hai" — Oil pressure light on.
  • "Battery ki warning aa gayi" — Alternator/battery warning.
  • "ABS ka light on hai" — ABS fault.
  • "Tyre mein hawa kam hai" — Low tyre pressure.
  • "Airbag light off nahi ho rahi" — Airbag warning stays on.
  • "Saari lights aik sath jal gayi" — All lights on at once (usually alternator).
