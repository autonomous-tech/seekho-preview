03

Licence process (DLS Sindh)

Yes — Pakistani law requires a valid learner's or permanent licence to drive, even while learning. A learner's licence costs Rs 100 and is issued by DLS Sindh at your nearest police centre. Bring your CNIC + 2 photos + medical fitness certificate. The learner's licence is valid for 6 months.
Three steps:
  1. Get a learner's licence (Rs 100, same-day issue with CNIC + medical).
  2. Wait 42 days minimum, practice driving.
  3. Book a theory + practical test at dls.gos.pk or dlsonline.sindhpolice.gov.pk.
Permanent licence fee: Rs 1,410 (5-year) or Rs 1,860 (10-year).
Seekho gives you the full DLS Sindh walkthrough on Day 1.
The DLS Sindh theory test has multiple-choice questions on road signs, right-of-way, speed limits, and basic road safety. Our Saturday workshop includes a mock theory test + downloadable practice sheets — most Seekho students pass on the first attempt.
DLS Sindh mandates a 42-day gap between issuing a learner's licence and testing for the permanent licence. This gives new drivers time to practice. It cannot legally be skipped. We time our packages to align with this gap — you finish training just as you become eligible to test.
Test day essentials: bring your learner's licence, CNIC, fee receipt, and your own car (or rent one from DLS). The examiner checks parking, lane changes, signal use, hill start, and basic road manners. Test lasts 10–15 minutes. Seekho can help you practice at the actual test routes in DHA Phase 6 and Phase 8.
You need:
  • Valid learner's or permanent driving licence (mandatory)
  • CNIC copy
  • Minimum age 18
  • For Student Bundle: valid school/university ID
That's it — we handle the rest.
