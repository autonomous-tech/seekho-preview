What is Traffic Club

A road-safety classroom — run by people who drive for a living.

Seekho Traffic Club (STC) is an initiative by Seekho to create awareness regarding road safety amongst children. Inspiring young minds to learn all about road safety is key to preventing accidents later on in life and helping them make better choices. Road safety is something that needs to be done continuously — and even subtly at times.

We run STC at schools, community centres, and neighbourhood societies across Karachi. The programme is built for children aged 6–14 and tailored to age group — younger kids focus on pedestrian safety and recognising signs; older kids go deeper into bicycle safety, emergency response, and what to do if you witness an accident. Every session is interactive, bilingual (Urdu + English), and taught by Seekho instructors who spend their days on Karachi's roads.

We cover basic road rules, pedestrian safety, bicycle safety, what to do in an emergency, and — the one most programmes skip — bystander responsibility. Kids need to know that if they see something unsafe, they can speak up. Teacher se bolo, parent ko bolo, 1122 call karo. Yeh cheezein bachpan se aati hain.

How We Teach

Four ways Traffic Club shows up.

Each format is free for partner schools — we cover costs through the Seekho main business.

🏫

School Workshops

Single-session or multi-week workshops inside the classroom or assembly hall. Age-appropriate slides, role-play, and take-home worksheets. Runs 45–90 minutes.

🏙

Community Days

Neighbourhood pop-ups in DHA, Clifton, and partner society spaces. Mock crossings, traffic-sign scavenger hunts, safety games — parents welcome.

👩‍🏫

Teacher Training

Half-day trainer-of-trainers session. We teach your staff to run the STC curriculum year-round — so road safety stops being a one-off assembly.

📖

Printed Materials

Colouring books with road rules, safety-sign flash cards, pedestrian-crossing posters. Free to partner schools. Useful well after the workshop ends.
